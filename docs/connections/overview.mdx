Tamery keeps all your database connections in one place, making it easy to switch between environments, share connection setups across devices, and stay organized as your project grows. Whether you're connecting to a local development database or a production cloud instance, the setup process takes just a few steps.

## Adding a Connection

To add a new database connection, click **Add new** on the Tamery dashboard and follow the three-step wizard.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose your database type">
    Select the database engine you want to connect to. Tamery supports PostgreSQL, MySQL, SQL Server (MSSQL), and ClickHouse.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your connection string">
    Paste or type your connection string into the provided field. Once you've entered it, click **Test Connection** to verify that Tamery can reach your database before you save anything.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name and configure your connection">
    Give your connection a memorable name, choose a sync type (see below), and optionally assign a label and color to help you stay organized. Click **Save** to finish.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Tamery tests your connection live before saving it. If the test fails, double-check your connection string — including the hostname, port, credentials, and database name — before proceeding.
</Note>

## Sync Types

Sync type controls whether Tamery stores your password alongside the rest of your connection details. Both options keep your connection available across all your devices.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Cloud (with password)">
    With **Cloud (with password)**, your full connection string — including the password — is encrypted with your personal secret key and stored in Tamery's cloud. The connection is automatically available on every device where you sign in to Tamery, with no need to re-enter your password.

    This option is the most convenient and is well-suited for development and staging databases where frictionless cross-device access is valuable.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Cloud (without password)">
    With **Cloud (without password)**, Tamery stores your connection metadata (host, port, database name, and username) in the cloud, but your password is **never stored**. You'll be prompted to enter the password each time you open the connection.

    This option is the right choice for production databases or any environment where your organization's security policy prohibits storing passwords in external systems.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Organizing Connections

As your list of connections grows, Tamery gives you a few tools to keep things tidy:

- **Labels** — attach a text label to a connection (for example, `production`, `staging`, or `personal`) to group and filter connections at a glance.
- **Colors** — assign a color to each connection so you can visually distinguish environments without reading the name.
- **Pinning** — pin your most-used connections to the top of the list for quick access.

You can update a connection's name, label, color, or sync type at any time from the connection settings.
